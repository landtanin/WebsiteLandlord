## SECTION 3: The Problem

## Why has spray foam insulation become a problem?

Spray foam was sold to thousands of Birmingham homeowners through the 2010s as an energy-efficiency upgrade — often under government grant schemes or doorstep sales. A decade on, the picture looks very different.

**Mortgage lenders have tightened up.** Many UK lenders now refuse to lend against properties with spray foam in the loft, particularly closed-cell foam applied directly to the underside of the roof. Others will consider it case-by-case, but expect a specialist survey, a removal quote, or both. If your remortgage was declined or your buyer's mortgage offer fell through, you're not alone — [read our guide to spray foam and mortgages](/guides/spray-foam-mortgage-problems).

**Surveyors flag it on home buyer reports.** Following [RICS guidance updated in 2021](/guides/rics-guidance-spray-foam), most surveyors now identify spray foam as a concern on valuation reports. That single line in a report can knock a meaningful sum off an offer or kill a sale outright.

**It can damage the roof it was meant to protect.** Open-cell foam in particular traps moisture against timbers. Ten-year-old installations have been reported with visible rot, sagging foam, and compromised roof felt. The longer it stays, the worse the underlying repair bill tends to get — [signs to watch for here](/guides/signs-spray-foam-needs-removal).

**It blocks the ventilation roofs were designed to have.** Traditional pitched roofs need to breathe. Spray foam — especially closed-cell — seals the roof structure, which can cause condensation problems further down the line.

## SECTION 5: The Removal Process

## How does spray foam removal work?

Here's how spray foam removal works. [The full process is here](/how-spray-foam-removal-works).

1. **Survey** — a specialist visits, identifies the foam type, photographs the loft, checks for moisture damage, and quotes
2. **Site preparation** — full PPE, dust sheeting, isolation of the loft from the rest of the home
3. **Manual removal** — foam is mechanically cut and scraped from rafters; this is the only method that works
4. **Disposal** — debris is bagged, removed from site, and sent to a licensed waste facility
5. **Structural inspection** — timbers are checked for rot, condensation damage, and ventilation reinstated
6. **Certification** — you receive photographs, a written report, and a certificate of completion

A typical semi-detached takes one to two days. Detached or large bungalows can take three. You can stay in the house throughout; the loft is sealed off from the living space.

## SECTION 7: Why act now

## Why does spray foam removal matter now?

If you're not planning to sell or remortgage, you might be wondering whether you can leave it. The honest answer is: probably, but the cost of waiting tends to grow rather than shrink.

**The lender market is moving against spray foam, not towards it.** RICS guidance and lender risk policies have tightened steadily since 2021. There's no realistic prospect of lenders softening their position; if anything, the next round of policy reviews is likely to be stricter.

**Property values are quietly affected even before a sale.** Estate agents we've spoken to suggest valuations can be materially affected — sometimes by tens of thousands — when spray foam is in the roof. Owners often find this out only when they try to sell.

**Damage compounds.** Moisture trapped against timber for an extra two or three years can turn a routine removal job into a removal-plus-repair job. The earlier you get a survey, the more options you have.

**Grants generally aren't coming.** Despite hopes that ECO4 or successor schemes would fund removal, very few homeowners qualify. [Our grants guide](/guides/spray-foam-grant-removal-options) explains where the exceptions are.

If you're planning to sell, remortgage, or release equity in the next five years, getting ahead of this is almost always the cheaper path. If you have no plans to move, the case is weaker — but worth a free conversation so you know what you're sitting on.

## SECTION 8: FAQ

## Common questions about spray foam removal

*Accessible accordion using semantic `<details>`/`<summary>` HTML. NO FAQPage JSON-LD.*

**Will my house sell if I have spray foam insulation?**
Often yes — but usually only to cash buyers, and usually at a reduced price. Most mortgage-backed buyers will struggle to get a lender to agree to the property as-is. Removing the foam before going to market typically widens your buyer pool and recovers more than the cost of removal in sale price. [More on selling with spray foam](/guides/selling-house-with-spray-foam).

**My mortgage lender refused — what now?**
Call us with the lender's response in hand — we can arrange a survey and quote that you can take back to your broker. Some specialist lenders will still consider spray foam properties case-by-case, particularly when a removal quote is on the table.

**How do I know if I have open-cell or closed-cell foam?**
Open-cell foam is soft and spongy — you can press a finger into it. Closed-cell is hard and rigid. Open-cell is usually white or pale; closed-cell is often yellow or honey-coloured. The distinction matters: closed-cell tends to be denser, more damaging to timbers when it fails, and slightly more expensive to remove.

**Is spray foam dangerous to live with?**
For most homeowners, day-to-day living with cured spray foam is low-risk. The bigger concerns are moisture damage to your roof structure and the mortgage/resale problems. [We cover the health evidence in detail here](/guides/spray-foam-health-risks).

**Can I remove it myself?**
We strongly advise against it. The dust contains residue that's an inhalation risk, the foam is mechanically bonded to your rafters in a way that's easy to damage, and councils generally won't take the waste. You also won't have certification to satisfy a lender. [Why DIY removal goes wrong](/guides/diy-spray-foam-removal-warning).

**How long does removal take?**
Most jobs are completed in one to two days. Larger or more complex roofs can take three. You can stay in your home throughout; the working area is fully isolated from the rest of the house.

**Are there grants available for removal?**
Generally, no. Spray foam removal isn't covered by ECO4 or the main retrofit schemes. There are narrow exceptions — some councils run small remediation funds, and if the original installer was at fault you may have an insurance claim. [Full grants guide here](/guides/spray-foam-grant-removal-options).

**What documentation do I get after removal?**
A written report, photographs of the before/after state, a structural inspection note, and a certificate of completion you can show your mortgage adviser, surveyor, or solicitor.
